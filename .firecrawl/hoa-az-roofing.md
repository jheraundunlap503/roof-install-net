# [What Your HOA Doesn’t Tell You About Roof Repairs & Replacements](https://www.jbsroofingaz.com/blog/what-to-know-hoa-roofing/)

[July 12, 2025](https://www.jbsroofingaz.com/blog/what-to-know-hoa-roofing/)

If you live in a community with a [homeowners association](https://www.investopedia.com/articles/mortgages-real-estate/08/homeowners-associations-tips.asp) (HOA), you’re probably used to getting approval for landscaping changes or exterior paint colors. But when it comes to your roof, many homeowners are caught off guard by just how much control their HOA has and how little guidance they offer until you’re already knee-deep in the process.

In Arizona, especially in 55+ communities and planned neighborhoods, roofing projects often fall under strict HOA regulations outlined in the community’s [CC&Rs](https://www.nar.realtor/magazine/real-estate-news/law-and-ethics/hoa-covenants) (Covenants, Conditions, and Restrictions). Whether you’re planning a roof repair, full replacement, or even minor cosmetic updates, it’s important to understand what your HOA requires and what they don’t always make obvious.

## Your Roof Might Be Subject to Strict Material Requirements

One of the most common HOA restrictions is around the type of roofing material allowed. Even if your roof is older or your contractor recommends a more modern or efficient material, the HOA may limit you to whatever was originally used in the community, [often tile or a specific color of shingle](https://www.jbsroofingaz.com/blog/roofing-materials-for-arizona/).

What you might not know:

- HOAs can reject materials even if they are better for energy efficiency or storm protection
- You may be required to match your neighbors’ roofs exactly in color, shape, and finish
- Some communities prohibit solar panels or require specific types that are less visible from the street

Before scheduling any roof work, it’s essential to review your CC&Rs and check for a list of approved materials or manufacturers. If that information isn’t clearly laid out, ask your HOA board or management company for clarification in writing.

## Approval Timelines Can Delay Repairs

Many HOAs require homeowners to submit detailed applications before approving [roof repairs](https://www.jbsroofingaz.com/residential/phoenix-roof-repair/) or replacements, especially if the work involves material changes, color adjustments, or structural modifications. Unfortunately, they don’t always tell you how long that process can take or what kind of documentation is needed.

Expect:

- Delays of several weeks or longer for HOA review and approval
- Requests for specific documentation like contractor license info, color samples, and product brochures
- Potential rejection if your submission lacks required details

If you’re dealing with an urgent issue, like a roof leak or storm damage, these delays can be incredibly frustrating. Some HOAs allow for emergency repairs without pre-approval, but not all. When in doubt, contact your board before moving forward.

## Cosmetic Changes Can Trigger Violations

It’s not just roofing material that matters. Paint color, fascia board updates, and even replacing visible flashing can fall under HOA oversight. Something as simple as replacing trim or edge metal in a slightly different color could result in a violation.

Be aware that:

- HOAs often require color matching for everything visible from the street
- Soffit vents, skylights, and solar tubes may also be regulated
- DIY repairs can lead to fines if they alter the appearance of your roof

To avoid headaches, always check whether your HOA has a pre-approved color palette or architectural guidelines. Even minor changes can be flagged if they affect your roofline or curb appeal.

## Navigating the Process Without Getting Stuck

HOA roofing projects don’t have to be painful, as long as you understand the process and work with a contractor who knows how to navigate it. At JBS Roofing, we’ve worked with countless HOAs across the Phoenix Valley and throughout Arizona, so we know how to streamline approvals and avoid unnecessary delays. We’re even the sole provider of roofing services for the HOAs in Sun City and Sun City West.

Tips for staying ahead of HOA red tape:

- Request the most current copy of your CC&Rs before planning any roof work
- Work with a roofing contractor experienced in HOA compliance
- Submit documentation early, including color samples, photos, product specs, and contractor info
- Get approvals in writing and keep records in case of disputes
- Don’t assume urgency exempts you from approval. Check emergency guidelines with the board

###### Final Thoughts

If you live in an HOA community, your roof isn’t entirely your decision… and that can be surprising, especially when repairs are time-sensitive or necessary for home safety. By understanding your HOA’s requirements and working with the right roofing partner, you can avoid delays, rejections, and costly violations.

At JBS Roofing, we’re happy to help homeowners and communities navigate the repair and replacement process from start to finish. If you’re unsure where to start, give us a call. We’ll walk you through it.
