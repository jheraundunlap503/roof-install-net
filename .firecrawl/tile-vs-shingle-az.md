# [Comparing Tile vs. Shingle Roofs: Which is Better for Scottsdale Homes?](https://www.azroofingworks.com/roof-tips/comparing-tile-vs-shingle-roofs/ "Permanent Link: Comparing Tile vs. Shingle Roofs: Which is Better for Scottsdale Homes?")

December 10, 2024/in [Roof Tips](https://www.azroofingworks.com/roof-tips/)/by [AZ Roofing Works](https://www.azroofingworks.com/author/az-roofing-works/ "Posts by AZ Roofing Works")

Despite their significant investment cost, there comes a time when you’ll need to consider replacing your roof.

While the lifespan of your roof depends on various factors, according to US News, you might want to explore getting a new roof once your current one is [20 years old](https://money.usnews.com/money/personal-finance/family-finance/articles/how-long-can-you-expect-your-roof-or-fridge-to-last).

Replacing your roof allows you to change the material if you choose to.

Scottsdale has a mostly dry climate, but the high temperatures throughout the summer may affect your roof.

Choosing the right roofing material impacts its durability and aesthetic appeal.

[**Comparing tile vs. shingle roofs**](https://www.azroofingworks.com/roof-repair/) is a great idea if you want the best value for your investment.

This blog will cover the basics of tile and shingle roofing for Scottsdale homes.

We’ll go over each type’s benefits, cost, and other elements so you can make the best choice for your home.

## What Is Shingle Roofing?

Shingle roofing is the _**most [common type of roofing material](https://www.nationwide.com/lc/resources/home/articles/types-of-roofing#:~:text=Asphalt%20shingles%20are%20the%20most,effective%20in%20all%20environmental%20conditions.)**_.

That’s primarily due to the fact they’re an effective and efficient choice for all environmental conditions.

One of the great things about this type of roofing is that they can be made from various materials, such as:

- Composites
- Slate
- Plastic
- Asphalt

Asphalt is the most frequently used material for these types of shingles.

Shingles are rectangular and small. They are installed in an overlapping pattern to keep out water.

Another thing to keep in mind with asphalt shingles is that they come in multiple colors and textures.

This makes them a versatile choice that will fit any home’s design style.

## What Is Tile Roofing?

Tile roofs have been around for hundreds of years.

They’re typically made from natural and recyclable materials, including:

- Terracotta
- Concrete
- Slate

_**Tile roofs are usually seen on homes that borrow design elements from Spanish architecture.**_

The tiles hang in parallel rows that overlap each other to keep out water.

Keep in mind that installing a tile roof is more intricate than other materials.

You need to work with an experienced company that has installed roofs like this to ensure the job is done correctly.

## What to Know When Comparing Tile vs. Shingle Roofs

There are a few things to consider when **comparing tile vs. shingle roofs**.

The cost, your home’s current design, and other factors need to play a role in your decision process.

### Tile vs. Shingle Cost

One of the main differences between the two kinds of roofs is that [tile roofs are more expensive](https://www.azroofingworks.com/roof-tips/what-influences-roof-installation-cost/) than shingle ones.

However, _**the life expectancy of tile roofs is much longer than shingle ones**_.

With that in mind, your investment works out to be about even.

Other factors that can affect the cost of your roof include:

- Roof size
- Labor costs
- Insulation and underlayment
- Permit cost

### Scottsdale Climate

Tile roofs can stand up to extremely high temperatures, making them the ideal choice for blistering Scottsdale summers.

Shingle roofs do provide your home with some protection against the climate in Scottsdale, but not as much as tile roofs.

Shingles can reflect intense sunlight, keeping the interior of your home cooler.

### Roof Weight

It’s important to keep in mind that not every roof can withstand the weight of tiles.

They’re extremely heavy, especially when compared to shingles.

_**Working with an experienced Scottsdale roof installation company can help you determine if your home can bear the weight of tiles.**_

### Maintenance Requirements

Our home’s roof, no matter what material it’s made from, takes a beating.

Some things that can cause issues with our roofs include:

- Poor installation
- Exposure to snow, hail, rain, and sunlight
- Plant or tree debris
- Inconsistent repairs or maintenance

_**It’s important to stay on top of cleaning and inspecting your roof for damage regardless of what type of roof you have.**_

We recommend cleaning your roof around three to four times a year, but more often if you’ve had a storm.

Regular maintenance is key to ensuring your roof lasts for many years.

With tile roofs, you need to stay on top of [replacing the underlayment](https://www.azroofingworks.com/roof-replacements/cost-to-replace-tile-roof-underlayment/) and broken tiles.

The underlayment is the material below the tiles that provide additional waterproofing and it doesn’t last as long as the tiles.

There are a few problems you may encounter with your shingle roof, such as:

- Curling
- Blistering
- Cracking
- Algae, mildew, and mold

Having an experienced roofing company check your roof will help you avoid serious problems from occurring.

### Sustainability

_**Tile roofs are considered a sustainable choice due to their energy efficiency and durability**_.

Concrete and clay are also natural materials, which makes them more environmentally friendly than other materials.

This includes asphalt shingles.

However, you can explore shingles made from recycled materials that are made from different things, including:

- Wood fiber
- Rubber
- Plastic

What’s great about these types of materials is that they divert waste from going into landfills.

They also decrease the need for raw materials for roofs.

Recycled shingles can give you a sustainable option if you’re wanting a shingle roof.

### Aesthetic Considerations

As we’ve talked about before, your home’s style will play a part in which type of roofing material you choose.

Tile roofing pairs wonderfully with homes that have a southwestern charm, which is prevalent throughout the Scottsdale area.

Tiles come in various colors and styles, making it easy for you to find an option that matches your home’s design.

Shingle roofs also come in [multiple colors](https://www.thespruce.com/what-paint-goes-with-a-brown-roof-8598079), such as:

- Blue
- Black
- White
- Grey
- Brown
- Green

If you’re unsure of what color to get, go with a more neutral color.

A brown or grey roof is a safe option, especially if you’re considering painting your home in the future.
